# Casting Library

Archetypes and rosters to pull from when auto-casting. Adapt names and details to the specific topic — these are starting points, not fixed casts.

## Universal archetypes (drop into almost any debate)

- **The Skeptic / Contrarian** — assumes it won't work; hunts the hidden flaw. Every debate needs one.
- **The Pragmatist** — "what actually ships / works on Monday?" Distrusts elegance for its own sake.
- **The Idealist / Purist** — argues from principle, long-term health, "do it right."
- **The Bean-Counter** — cost, ROI, opportunity cost, lock-in. Unmoved by excitement.
- **The End User / Customer** — doesn't care about internals; cares about their own outcome.
- **The Operator** — who maintains/supports/runs it after launch? Bears the on-call pain.
- **The Optimist / Champion** — genuine believer; makes the strongest case FOR. Not a yes-man — a true advocate.
- **The Veteran** — "we tried this in 2014." Pattern-matches to past failures and successes.

## Domain rosters

**Tech stack / architecture decision**
Pragmatic senior engineer · Startup CTO (velocity-first) · SRE/ops (reliability, on-call) · Hype-skeptic · (optional) the framework's true believer.

**Product evaluation / critique**
Power user · Casual/non-technical user · Economic buyer (budget, ROI) · Competitor's loyal customer (switching cost) · Accessibility/edge-case user.

**Pricing / monetization**
Growth lead (acquisition, LTV) · Customer advocate (churn, fairness) · CFO (margin) · Front-line sales (deals at risk) · A price-sensitive customer.

**Go-to-market / strategy**
Founder/visionary · Head of sales (pipeline reality) · Marketing (positioning) · Skeptical board member · Target-customer stand-in.

**Red-team a plan / launch**
Security & abuse angle · Scale/performance angle · Legal/compliance/PR angle · Dependency/vendor-risk angle · The plan's author (defender).

**Hiring / org / process**
Manager (delivery) · IC (day-to-day reality) · HR/people (fairness, retention) · Finance (headcount cost) · The skeptic.

**Build vs buy**
Engineer who'd build it (control, fit) · Engineer who'd buy it (time-to-value) · Finance (TCO) · Security (vendor risk) · The user who just wants it now.

**Design / UX direction**
Designer (craft, consistency) · Engineer (feasibility, effort) · PM (scope, deadline) · User (clarity) · Brand/marketing (distinctiveness).

## Casting anti-patterns (avoid these)

- **Twins.** Two personas with the same incentive in different costumes. Merge or recast one with opposed interests.
- **Strawman.** A persona who exists only to be obviously wrong. Every persona must be defensible; the debate is boring if one side is a punching bag.
- **The committee.** Five personas who all basically agree. No friction = no debate. Cut to the ones who actually conflict.
- **Label without motive.** "The CFO" with no specified fear or optimization target argues generically. Always give the motive + the fear.
- **Omniscient persona.** A persona who happens to hold the moderator's view and "wins." Stay neutral; let positions fight.

## Tuning the cast

- **Sharpen conflict:** give two personas directly competing success metrics (one rewarded for speed, one for safety).
- **Add a wildcard** for exploratory debates: an outsider who reframes the question entirely.
- **Match count to topic richness:** binary decision → 2–3; multi-stakeholder strategy → 4–5.
